# FundLocal — AI Community Investment Platform

## What can humans now do that they couldn't before?

Today, a Wealthsimple user who cares about their community has two options: invest in ETFs and hope for the best, or donate to causes and never hear what happened. There's no way to say "I want to fund the solar project on my neighbourhood school's roof" and actually do it. The discovery is fragmented across municipal budgets, GoFundMe pages, and grant applications. The evaluation requires expertise most people don't have. The transaction rails don't exist. And there's zero accountability — you get a tax receipt, not proof the microscopes were purchased.

FundLocal connects everyday investors with community projects — school upgrades, local solar installations, small business micro-loans, wetland restoration, scientific research — matched by AI to their values, location, and return preferences. It also serves the other side: project creators get AI evaluation that coaches their proposals toward fundability, replacing the grant writing consultant they can't afford.

The result: a parent council raising $45K for a science lab and the investor three blocks away who'd fund it can finally find each other.

## What is the AI responsible for?

The AI does two distinct cognitive jobs.

**For investors**, it matches across fuzzy dimensions that can't be reduced to filters. It reads unstructured project proposals, evaluates feasibility and credibility, scores alignment against the investor's values profile, and generates natural language explanations for why each project fits — or doesn't. This is judgment work, not search.

**For project creators**, it evaluates raw proposals against four dimensions — impact potential, feasibility, readiness, and clarity — then provides specific, actionable coaching tied to what's missing from *their* proposal. "Your timeline is aggressive given the permitting requirements" is different from generic advice. The AI acts as a grant writing consultant that scales to every community organization in the country.

## Where does the AI stop?

At three explicit gates.

First, the AI evaluates creator proposals but never approves them for listing. A human compliance review verifies identity, confirms the organization exists, and ensures regulatory requirements are met before any project reaches investors.

Second, the AI matches and recommends but never invests. The investor sees the reasoning, the score breakdown, and the limitations — then decides. Choosing between funding a school or a solar project is a values judgment only the human can make. The system is designed so that removing this gate would break the value proposition entirely.

Third, the AI surfaces what it can't verify. Each project shows explicit limitations: "We have not independently verified the cost estimates" or "Research outcomes are inherently unpredictable." The system doesn't pretend to be omniscient.

## What breaks first at scale?

Outcome verification. Confirming that a school bought microscopes is straightforward — receipts and photos. Confirming that a wetland restoration improved biodiversity requires multi-year ecological assessment that AI can't do alone. FundLocal starts with projects where verification is simple and expands as methods mature — but this is the honest constraint, and the primary reason human oversight can't be removed from the system.
