# FundLocal — Written Explanation

## What can people do now that they couldn't before?

Put $500 into a local community project and have it be a real investment — structured, tracked, and accountable — not a donation and a prayer. Before this, it was technically possible but practically unrealistic. You'd need connections to find projects, expertise to assess them, and blind trust that they'd follow through.

Three things are now possible that weren't. First, personalized matching with natural language reasoning — not a filtered list, but AI that explains *why* a project fits your values and portfolio mix in plain language. Second, structured accountability — creators commit to milestones, funds release against verified progress, investors track impact over time. Third, AI-assisted compliance that makes small projects economically viable. Without AI pre-screening and credential verification, the cost of reviewing a $35,000 community garden would make it unfundable. AI brings that cost down to where community-sized investments work.

## What is AI responsible for?

The analytical work that no filter could handle. On the investor side, the AI matches values against unstructured project descriptions, reasons about portfolio-level complementarity, and writes plain-language explanations for every recommendation. On the creator side, it evaluates proposals against credibility, readiness, and feasibility — flagging unverified claims separately from general risk, and generating project-specific compliance checklists that push creators to provide evidence before human review begins.

Behind the scenes, the architecture extends AI into operational review — the same pattern that applies to any human review process: surfacing credential inconsistencies, cross-referencing public records, pre-populating background check inputs, and prioritizing the queue by risk level — reducing hours of reviewer work per project to seconds.

## Where must AI stop?

Three hard boundaries. First, the AI never moves money — it recommends, the investor decides. Second, no project reaches investors without human compliance review. The AI pre-screens proposals and flags what it can't verify — but a human confirms every approval. When the AI and a human reviewer disagree, the human decision stands. The AI's assessment stays in the audit trail and feeds back into future evaluations, but never overrides a human call. Third, the AI is never a single point of failure. If an API call fails, the app surfaces a clear error, offers retry, and deterministic logic populates baseline results.

## What breaks first at scale?

Project verification. The AI can pre-screen thousands of proposals per day. A human compliance team cannot. Every project needs someone to confirm the charity registration is real, the land access claim holds up, the credentials check out. AI helps — generating tailored checklists, surfacing inconsistencies, cross-referencing public records, and triaging the queue so reviewers focus where risk is highest. But the final call is human, and that's a funnel constraint. From 50 to 5,000 projects, AI scales linearly while human review scales with headcount. The mitigation is AI tooling that makes each reviewer dramatically more efficient — reducing hours-per-project to minutes-per-project so the bottleneck widens before it breaks.
