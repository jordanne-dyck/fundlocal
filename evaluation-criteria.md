# Wealthsimple AI Builder — Evaluation Criteria

## What they're looking for in a person

- Thinks big, sweats the small stuff, keeps things simple
- Speed, judgment, and ownership
- Thinks in systems, moves across disciplines, turns ambiguity into shipped work
- Mix of product thinker, engineer, operator, and strategist
- Prototypes quickly, operates end-to-end
- Coordinates complex cross-functional work without losing momentum
- Hands-on experience building with AI tools, models, or APIs
- Cares whether something would actually work in the real world — not just in a demo
- Energized by change, leans into new technology
- AI-first thinking: reflexively uses AI to expand what's possible

## What they're looking for in the build

### The system must:
1. Clearly define the human's role
2. Take on real cognitive or operational responsibility
3. Explicitly name one critical decision that must remain human — and why

### Demo video (2-3 minutes):
- Show the system actually working

### Written explanation (max 500 words):
- What the human can now do that they couldn't before
- What AI is responsible for
- Where AI must stop
- What would break first at scale

## How they evaluate

- Sound judgment and systems that would hold up in the real world
- Choosing a real problem
- Clearly defining the human/AI boundary
- Showing meaningful AI responsibility
- How you handle uncertainty
- Pressure test failure modes
- Design for scale
- Communicate reasoning clearly
- AI-first thinking

## The role itself

- Not a traditional role — small team of cross-functional builders
- Rethink how modern AI should change how work gets done
- Rebuild business processes from scratch as AI-native systems (not layering AI onto old workflows)
- Own the full path from problem to shipped system to real-world adoption
- Work alongside an experienced AI leader and domain experts
- Ship systems that handle real cognitive and operational responsibility, at scale

### Day-to-day:
- Identify business processes that evolved before modern AI and redesign them as AI-native workflows
- Prototype quickly, test assumptions, iterate based on operational reality
- Work across functions: engineering, regulatory & compliance, go-to-market
- Collaborate with domain experts to pressure-test ideas
- Make explicit decisions about where AI should take on responsibility — and where it shouldn't
- You bring the judgment, not execute someone else's roadmap
